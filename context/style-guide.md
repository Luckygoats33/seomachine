# Nature's Turn Style Guide

This guide defines writing conventions, formatting standards, and editorial guidelines for all Nature's Turn content.

---

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Capitalization

**Headlines & Subheadings**:
- **Decision**: Title Case (Capitalize Major Words)

**Brand & Product Names**:
- Nature's Turn: Always capitalized, always with apostrophe (never "Natures Turn" or "Nature's turn")
- Product names: "Strawberry Crisps," "Mango Crisps," "Tropical Blend," "Apple Cinnamon" — capitalize both words
- Freeze-dried: Hyphenated, lowercase unless starting a sentence
- Non-GMO Project Verified: Capitalize as shown (official certification name)

**Industry Terms**:
- freeze-dried / Freeze-dried: Lowercase with hyphen (capitalize at sentence start only)
- crisps: Always lowercase
- Kosher: Capitalize (proper noun in religious context)
- non-GMO: Lowercase "non," uppercase "GMO"

### Numbers

**When to Spell Out**:
- Spell out: One through nine
- Use numerals: 10 and above
- Exceptions:
  - Percentages: Always use numerals (5%, 97%)
  - Money: Always use numerals ($13.99, $500)
  - Measurements: Always use numerals (2 oz, 28g)
  - Nutrition facts: Always use numerals (25mg vitamin C, 3g fiber)
  - Lists/stats: Always use numerals for scannability

**Large Numbers**:
- 1,000+ (use comma)
- 1 million (spell out million, billion, etc.)
- $5,000 or $5K: Use $5K for casual content, $5,000 for formal

### Punctuation

**Oxford Comma**:
- **Decision**: Yes (strawberries, bananas, and mangoes)

**Em Dashes**:
- Style: — (em dash, no spaces)
- Usage: For parenthetical statements or emphasis
- Example: "Our freeze-dried crisps—made from 100% fruit—are the cleanest snack in your pantry."

**Quotation Marks**:
- **Decision**: Straight quotes in digital content

**Ellipses**:
- Three dots: ...
- Spacing: No spaces before or after
- Use sparingly: Primarily for omitted text in quotes

### Abbreviations & Acronyms

**First Use**:
- Spell out on first use, acronym in parentheses
- Example: "Non-GMO Project Verified (Non-GMO)" on first use, then "Non-GMO" after
- Exceptions for universally known: SEO, URL, FAQ, USDA

**Common Industry Acronyms**:
- GMO: Genetically Modified Organism
- USDA: United States Department of Agriculture
- FDA: Food and Drug Administration
- SEO: Search Engine Optimization
- D2C: Direct-to-Consumer
- CPG: Consumer Packaged Goods

**Latin Abbreviations**:
- **Decision**: Generally avoid in favor of plain English. Use "for example" instead of "e.g." and "that is" instead of "i.e." in consumer-facing content. Acceptable in technical/SEO-focused content.

## Word Choice & Usage

### Preferred Terms

**Say This** → **Not That**:
- freeze-dried crisps → chips (crisps conveys premium, light texture)
- freeze-dried fruit → dried fruit (distinct process — never conflate)
- no sugar added → sugar-free (legally distinct; fruit has natural sugars)
- nutrient-dense → healthy (too vague and potentially non-compliant)
- single-ingredient → simple or clean (be specific)
- pouch → bag (our packaging format)
- fruit crisps → fruit snacks (differentiates from gummy "fruit snacks")
- allergen-free facility → hypoallergenic (inaccurate for food)
- Non-GMO Project Verified → non-GMO (use full certification name)
- naturally sweet → sweet (clarify the source)

### Words to Avoid
- "All-natural" (vague, unregulated, potentially misleading)
- "Superfood" (overused, no regulated definition)
- "Guilt-free" (implies guilt about other food choices — judgmental)
- "Clean eating" as a noun (use "clean-label" or "clean ingredients" instead)
- "Processed" to describe our product (technically true but carries negative connotation — use "freeze-dried")
- "Very," "really," "actually" (usually unnecessary filler)
- "Click here" or "read more" (use descriptive link text)

### Inclusive Language
- Use gender-neutral language ("they" instead of "he/she")
- "Parents" and "families" instead of "moms" (unless specifically targeting mothers)
- Avoid idioms that may not translate globally
- Be mindful of accessibility in descriptions

## Formatting Standards

### Text Formatting

**Bold**:
- Use for: Key concepts, product names in feature lists, important takeaways
- Don't overuse: If everything is bold, nothing stands out
- Example: "**No sugar added** — just the natural sweetness of real fruit."

**Italics**:
- Use for: Emphasis (sparingly), titles of publications, scientific terms on first use
- Example: "The process of *sublimation* removes water without heat."

**Underline**:
- Generally avoid (reserve for links)

**ALL CAPS**:
- Avoid except for standard acronyms (GMO, SEO, USDA, FAQ)

### Lists

**Bulleted Lists**:
- Use for: Non-sequential items, features, benefits, product varieties
- Capitalization: Capitalize first word
- Punctuation: Period if complete sentence, no period if fragment
- Parallel structure required (all sentences or all fragments)

**Numbered Lists**:
- Use for: Sequential steps, rankings, ordered priorities
- Format: 1. 2. 3. (with period after number)
- Same capitalization and punctuation rules as bullets

**Nested Lists**:
- Maximum 2 levels deep for readability
- Use different markers for sub-items

### Links

**Anchor Text**:
- Descriptive (tell reader where they're going)
- Keyword-rich when possible
- 2–5 words typically
- Good: "learn more about freeze-dried fruit nutrition"
- Bad: "click here"

**Link Formatting**:
- Open in new tab: Only for external links
- Internal links: Open in same tab

### Callout Boxes / Asides

**When to Use**:
- Important nutrition notes or allergen information
- Pro tips for using products
- Key takeaways or summaries

**Format**:
- Use blockquote styling or highlighted box
- Label with: "Pro Tip:", "Did You Know:", "Nutrition Note:", "Important:"

## Content Structure

### Article Introduction
**Standard Structure** (150–250 words):
1. **Hook** (1–2 sentences): Grab attention with a question, surprising stat, or relatable scenario
2. **Problem** (2–3 sentences): What challenge or question does this address?
3. **Promise** (2–3 sentences): What will the reader learn or gain?
4. **Credibility** (optional, 1–2 sentences): Why trust Nature's Turn on this topic?

**Keyword Placement**:
- Must include primary keyword in first 100 words
- Natural integration, not forced

### Section Length
- **Minimum**: 150 words per section
- **Maximum**: 500 words per section (break into subsections if longer)
- **Ideal**: 250–350 words per main section

### Conclusion
**Standard Structure** (150–250 words):
1. **Recap** (3–5 bullet points or paragraph): Key takeaways
2. **Action** (1–2 sentences): What should the reader do next?
3. **CTA** (1–2 sentences): Shop products, explore recipes, learn more
4. **Forward-looking** (optional, 1 sentence): Encouraging final thought

## SEO-Specific Style

### Meta Titles
- 50–60 characters including spaces
- Include primary keyword
- Include "| Nature's Turn" if space allows
- No ending punctuation

### Meta Descriptions
- 150–160 characters including spaces
- Include primary keyword
- Include call-to-action
- End with complete thought

### URL Slugs
- Lowercase only
- Hyphens between words
- Include primary keyword
- 3–5 words ideal
- Format: `/blog/primary-keyword-phrase`

### Alt Text
- Describe what image shows
- Include keyword naturally if relevant
- 125 characters or less
- No "image of" or "picture of" (implied)

## Dates & Time

**Date Format**:
- **Decision**: Month DD, YYYY (February 28, 2026)

**Time**:
- **Decision**: 12-hour format with a.m./p.m.: 3:00 p.m.

**Time Zones**:
- Specify when relevant: "3:00 p.m. ET"

---

## Statistics & Data

### Citing Sources
- Always cite statistics with sources
- Format: "According to [Source], [statistic]."
- Link to original source when possible
- Include year of data: "In 2025, the freeze-dried fruit market reached..."

### Presenting Numbers
- Round large numbers for readability: "1.2 million" not "1,234,567"
- Use % symbol: 15% (not "percent")
- Use $ for money: $13.99 (not "dollars")
- Include commas: 10,000 not 10000

---

## Images & Media

### Image Captions
- **Decision**: Optional, only when needed for context

**Caption Style**:
- Sentence case
- End with period if complete sentence
- Placement: Below image

### Screenshots
- Include product photography when available
- Crop to show relevant product detail
- Add annotations if needed for how-to content
- Alt text should describe what's shown

## Brand-Specific Guidelines

### Nature's Turn Product References

**Brand Name**:
- "Nature's Turn" (not "the Nature's Turn brand" unless needed for clarity)
- Never abbreviate to "NT"
- Always include the apostrophe

**Products**:
- "[Fruit] Crisps" — capitalize both words: "Strawberry Crisps," "Mango Crisps"
- "[Blend Name]" — capitalize: "Tropical Blend," "Apple Cinnamon"
- Generic reference: "freeze-dried fruit crisps" (lowercase when not a specific product name)

**Certifications**:
- Non-GMO Project Verified (capitalize, always include "Project Verified")
- Kosher Certified (capitalize both)
- "Made in an allergen-free facility" (lowercase "allergen-free facility")

### Competitor References
- **Decision**: Name competitors directly when relevant for comparison content

**If Naming Competitors**:
- Be fair and factual
- No disparaging language
- Focus on differentiation, not criticism
- Always verify competitor claims are current

## Accessibility

### Screen Reader Friendly
- Descriptive link text (not "click here")
- Image alt text for all images
- Proper heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3, no skipping)
- Descriptive link previews

### Plain Language
- Write at 8th–10th grade level
- Define technical terms on first use (e.g., sublimation, freeze-drying)
- Use short sentences and paragraphs
- Break complex ideas into digestible chunks

## Voice & Tone Reminders

### Core Voice Characteristics
1. Approachable and warm
2. Health-first and evidence-based
3. Family-friendly and trustworthy
4. Transparent and honest
5. Enthusiastic about real fruit and clean ingredients

### Tone Variations
- **How-to/recipe content**: Encouraging, practical, fun
- **Nutrition/health content**: Authoritative, clear, evidence-based
- **Brand story content**: Heartfelt, purpose-driven, authentic
- **Product content**: Benefit-focused, sensory, inviting
